About

This project involved the installation and configuration of a Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS) for a growing small fictional business, enabling the collection of network traffic and implementation of rules for enhanced network security. This project provided valuable hands-on experience in network security and rule-based NIDS implementation.

Project Overview

Barker Labs, a clinical research lab that has developed a comprehensive vaccine for COVID-19, has gained prominence due to its unique attributes. Unlike other vaccines that require multiple doses, fail to protect against related variants, and may cause significant side effects or unforeseen complications, Barker Labs' one-time vaccine prevents COVID-19 and all its relative variants. While currently undergoing human testing, the vaccine has already garnered attention amidst competition.

Barker Labs, founded two years ago, has experienced steady growth alongside its clinical research endeavors. However, their Information Technology (IT) Department has relied on the same Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS) for the past two years. Initially, threats were not a major concern due to the company's small size and the availability of other competitors' findings and vaccines in the market. Nevertheless, the existing NIDS is now outdated and incapable of detecting emerging threats or providing valuable insights to enhance other cybersecurity components.

To address these challenges, a new NIDS system will be implemented and configured, empowering the IT Department to make informed decisions regarding additional preventive measures that can bolster the network security of the company.

Project Purpose

This project served as my capstone for my undergraduate studies and provided me with valuable insights into network security. It allowed me to delve into the intricacies of implementing rules for the NIDS, enabling it to effectively collect and analyze network traffic.

During the project, I gained knowledge about various network security concepts, such as intrusion detection and prevention, as well as the importance of creating specific rules tailored to the business's needs. These rules were instrumental in ensuring that the NIDS efficiently captured relevant network traffic, providing a solid foundation for monitoring and identifying potential security threats.

Engaging in this capstone project not only broadened my understanding of network security principles but also equipped me with practical skills in configuring and optimizing an NIDS to meet specific requirements. It was a rewarding experience that enhanced my academic journey and prepared me for future endeavors in the field of network security.

Project Implementation Details

For this project, I utilized Snort as the Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS). Snort is an open-source solution renowned for its flexibility and effectiveness in detecting and preventing network intrusions. Its open-source nature significantly reduced costs, making it an economical choice for the project.

I chose Snort because of its robust intrusion detection capabilities and its ability to be customized with specific rules tailored to the business's security needs. This made it an ideal tool for analyzing network traffic and detecting potential threats.

In terms of infrastructure, I used Virtual Machines to simulate the fictional business's Linux-based systems. Specifically, I deployed the Ubuntu distribution within these VMs. Additionally, I utilized my localhost, which runs Windows, to facilitate various aspects of the configuration and testing process.

Overall, Snort's features, combined with the use of Virtual Machines and my local environment, allowed for a comprehensive and effective implementation of the NIDS.

Project Report

I chose Snort as the Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS) due to its open-source nature, which helped reduce costs. Snort offers comprehensive protection by functioning as both an intrusion detection and prevention system, identifying potential threats and taking action to prevent them. To simulate the fictional business environment, I used Virtual Machines with the Ubuntu Linux distribution and my local Windows host system.
